DedSec UI design (personal project)


Objective

My objective for this project was to develop a visual UI concept for the operatives/team system in Watch Dogs: Legion.
The reason behind this initiative stemmed from my belief that the current UI failed to encapsulate the true aesthetic
essence of the Watch Dogs series. I aimed to craft something more engaging and representative of the hacker group, DedSec.
For inspiration, my focus was directed towards capturing the distinct personality of DedSec and the intricate cyberpunk universe
inhabited by these characters. This inspired me to create the screen layout with the intention of enabling players to navigate through
it using a cursor-style selection and traditional gamepad controls. The aesthetic I strived for was deeply rooted in the cyberpunk ethos,
characterised by its vibrant use of gloomy/neon colours, specific shape language, and the incorporation of CRT textures. I aimed to infuse
the UI with these elements, ensuring it would resonate with the cyberpunk atmosphere while aligning with the playful spirit of DedSec.

Shape language

The shape language for this project drew inspiration from a floppy disk. My inspiration came from its purpose, which is used for storing information,
this served as my creative influence for the design, infusing a touch of retro-futurism into the interface. The angular corners and geometric simplicity
of the floppy disk's form played a huge role in shaping the visual identity of the UI, offering a subtle nod to the technological
relics of the past while seamlessly integrating them into the cyberpunk aesthetics.

gloomy/neon

The colours I utilised were intended to evoke the atmosphere of a neon-soaked, dystopian future, blurring the line between the gloomy cityscape and the vivid neon lights.

Type

  • blender pro

    • Used for majority of the project, different weights were implemented for better type heirachy. for tittles i specifically
    used heavy. For body text i mainly used medium which was a suitable weight for reading from a far.

  • Orbitron

    • Orbitron black was mainly used for numbers, specifically stats, and inventory amounts as i felt it was more impactful then blender pro when displaying numbers or singular units. Was used for controller inputs as well.
